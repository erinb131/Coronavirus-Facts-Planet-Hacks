How to Protect Yourself

How COVID-19 is Spread

The virus is thought to spread mainly from person to person.

  • Through respiratory droplets produced when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.
  • These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby or possibly be inhaled into the lungs.
  • COVID-19 may be spread by people who are not showing symptoms.

Other ways that the virus can be spread include:

  • Touching a surface/object that has the virus on it, and then touching your own mouth, nose, or possibly your eyes
  • From animals to people, however, the risk is considered to be low
  • From people to animals

Protection Methods

Clean and Disinfect

  • Clean high-touch surfaces using soap and water (to reduce number of germs, dirt, and impurities) and then disinfecting (to kill germs)
  • To disinfect, use EPA-registered household disinfectant, diluted household bleach solutions (if appropriate for the surface), or alcohol solutions with at least 70% alcohol
  • Either wash your hands for at least 20 seconds, or use hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol

  • Stay out of crowded places and avoid mass gatherings
  • Follow guidance from authorities where you live

Cloth Face Coverings (Masks)/Gloves

  • Wear a mask in public settings where other social distancing measures are difficult to maintain (grocery stores, pharmacies, gas stations, etc.)
  • Masks may slow the spread of the virus and help people who may have the virus and do not know it (asymptomatic) from transmitting it to others
  • Masks can be made from household items (bandana, old t-shirt, square cotton cloth)
  • Children under age 2 or people who have trouble breathing, are unconscious, incapacitated, or otherwise unable to remove the mask without assistance, should not wear a mask
  • Wear gloves when cleaning and disinfecting your home as well as when caring for someone who is sick
