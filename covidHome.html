Coronavirus Facts

This website will provide you with facts regarding the current pandemic of coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19), using some of the most important information collected from a trusted source: the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). After reviewing the information, then you will be able to test your knowledge completing a quiz.

Main Facts

  • COVID-19, along with other diseases, can affect anyone, regardless of their race or ethnicity. Therefore, you should not allow any fear or anxiety surrounding COVID-19 to make you avoid or reject others, even though they aren’t at risk for spreading the virus.
  • People who have serious underlying medical conditions, along with older adults, may be at higher risk for more serious complications from COVID-19. However, the immediate risk of becoming seriously ill from the virus is thought to be low.
  • You will not be at risk of becoming infected if you have come into contact with someone who has completed quarantine or has been released from isolation.
  • Things that you can do to keep yourself and others healthy include:
    • Wash your hands with soap and water for a minimum of 20 seconds, especially if you do one of these things: blow your nose, cough, sneeze, go to the bathroom, or eat/prepare food.
    • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth if you have not washed your hands.
    • If you are sick, stay home.
    • If you are going to cough or sneeze, cover it with a tissue, and then throw the tissue in the trash (and don’t forget to wash your hands!)
  • The main signs and symptoms of COVID-19 include fever, cough, and shortness of breath, so you should seek medical attention if you develop these symptoms, OR if
    • You have been in close contact with someone who you know has COVID-19
    • You have recently traveled from an area with ongoing spread of COVID-19
